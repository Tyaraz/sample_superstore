# Superstore Sales & Profitability Analysis (2014–2017)

## 📌 Overview
An analysis of **9,994 retail orders** across four years and four geographic regions to identify revenue trends, isolate profitability drivers, and deliver actionable business recommendations.

## 🛠️ Tools Used
* **Language/Libraries:** Python (`pandas`, `matplotlib`)
* **Environment:** Jupyter Notebook via Kaggle
* **Dataset:** Sample Superstore (Kaggle)

## 🎯 Business Questions Addressed
1. How does revenue grow year over year?
2. Which region generates the most revenue and profit?
3. Which category and sub-category is the most profitable?
4. Does discounting increase or destroy profit margins?
5. Which specific sub-categories lose money despite generating high sales?

## 📊 Key Findings
* **Revenue grew 51.4%** from 2014 to 2017, but the average order value declined.
* **The Central region underperforms** significantly, sitting at a low 7.9% margin due to an aggressive 24% average discount.
* **Furniture generates 32.3% of total revenue** but contributes a meager 2.5% to overall profit margins.
* **Discounts above 30%** are highly destructive, collectively losing the business **$135,375**.
* **Tables sell consistently below cost**, running at a 108.6% cost ratio in 3 out of 4 regions.

## 💡 Recommendations

| Problem Identified | Data-Driven Recommendation |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Destructive Discounting** | Cap promotional discounts at a maximum of 20% to prevent the $135,375 margin bleed. |
| **Underperforming Central Region** | Restructure regional pricing rules and scale back the 24% average discount baseline. |
| **Furniture & Tables Drag** | Revise logistics contracts for heavy furniture and adjust the baseline retail price for Tables. |
| **Declining Order Value** | Implement cross-selling strategies or product bundling to reverse the drop in average order value. |
